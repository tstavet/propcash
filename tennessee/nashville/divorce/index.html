Understanding Divorce and Property Division in Nashville

Tennessee is an equitable distribution state, meaning marital property (including your home) is divided fairly but not necessarily equally in a divorce. For many Nashville couples, the family home represents their largest shared asset and often the most emotional one. According to Tennessee family law, the court considers factors like the length of marriage, each spouse's economic circumstances, contributions to the property's acquisition, and the needs of any minor children when determining property division.

Common Divorce Home Sale Scenarios in Nashville

  • Court-ordered sales: The divorce decree may require the home to be sold with proceeds divided per the settlement agreement
  • Buyout situations: One spouse may need to sell to access their equity for buying out the other's share
  • Cannot afford payments: Neither spouse can afford the mortgage independently, necessitating a quick sale
  • Need immediate liquidity: Legal fees, establishing separate households, and other divorce costs require quick access to home equity
  • Emotional closure: Both parties want to sell the marital home to achieve emotional closure and start fresh

Important: We are not attorneys and cannot provide legal advice. We recommend consulting with a Tennessee divorce attorney regarding your specific property division situation. We're happy to work with your legal counsel throughout the process.

Frequently Asked Questions About Selling During Divorce

Can you buy our Nashville home if we're going through a divorce?

Absolutely! We regularly work with divorcing couples in Nashville who need to sell their marital home quickly. We understand the sensitivity of your situation and can work with both parties (or their attorneys) to ensure a fair, transparent transaction that meets your divorce settlement requirements.

Do both spouses need to agree to sell?

In most cases, yes — if both spouses are on the title, both generally need to agree to the sale. However, situations vary, especially if there's a court order. We recommend discussing this with your divorce attorney. We're happy to work with your legal counsel to structure the sale appropriately.

What if only one of us is on the mortgage or title?

Property division in divorce can be complex when ownership is not equal. Tennessee is an equitable distribution state, so the court will determine fair division even if only one spouse is on the title. We recommend working with your attorney on this, and we can coordinate with them to execute the sale correctly.

How do you handle distributing the proceeds between both parties?

At closing, we follow the instructions provided by you and your attorneys. Proceeds can be split according to your divorce settlement agreement, held in escrow pending final decree, or distributed as directed by the court. We work directly with your legal counsel to ensure everything is handled correctly.

Can we do separate property walk-throughs?

Yes! We understand this is a difficult time and are happy to accommodate separate property visits if that's more comfortable for both parties. We'll assess the property during one or both visits and provide our cash offer.

How quickly can you close on a divorce property sale?

We can close in as little as 7-14 days, which is often crucial for divorcing couples who need to finalize their separation quickly. If your divorce proceedings require more time, we can also accommodate a later closing date that works with your court timeline.

What if one spouse still lives in the house?

Not a problem. If one spouse is still occupying the property, we'll work around their schedule for the walk-through and closing. The occupying spouse will need to vacate by the closing date, but we can provide flexible timelines to accommodate moving arrangements.

Do we need to make repairs or clean the house before selling?

No! This is one of the biggest benefits of selling to us during a divorce. We buy the property as-is — no repairs, no cleaning, no staging. This eliminates one more thing you and your ex-spouse would have to coordinate and pay for.

What if we're behind on mortgage payments?

We can often help Nashville couples who are behind on mortgage payments due to divorce-related financial strain. If you're facing foreclosure, selling quickly for cash may help you avoid that on your credit report. Contact us to discuss your specific situation.

Are your services confidential?

Yes, completely. We understand the sensitive nature of divorce and treat all inquiries and transactions with the utmost discretion and professionalism. Your privacy is important to us.

Will the sale be public record?

Like all real estate transactions in Tennessee, the final sale will be recorded with the county. However, unlike a traditional listing, your property won't be advertised online with photos, there won't be a "For Sale" sign in your yard, and there won't be multiple showings with neighbors watching. We keep the process as private as possible.

What areas of Nashville do you serve for divorce property sales?

We buy homes throughout all of Nashville and Davidson County in any divorce situation. This includes East Nashville, Green Hills, 12 South, The Gulch, Germantown, Belle Meade, Donelson, Antioch, Hermitage, Madison, and all other Nashville neighborhoods.
