Understanding Antioch's real estate market

The rental property exit strategy

Roughly 40% of Antioch's housing stock is investor-owned rentals. If you're one of the landlords who bought during the 2010s boom and you're now tired of property management headaches, you're not alone. Rising maintenance costs, difficult tenants, and the hassle of being an out-of-state owner are driving many Antioch landlords to sell. Much of Antioch's housing was built between 1970-1995. These homes often need significant updates — original HVAC systems, aging roofs, outdated electrical panels. Our investor network includes buyers who specialize in exactly these properties, and they price their offers knowing renovation costs upfront.

Why "affordable" doesn't mean "lowball"

Yes, Antioch has lower home prices than East Nashville or Green Hills. But lower prices attract more investors, not fewer — and more investors means more competition for your property. The "we buy houses" companies targeting Antioch know many sellers feel they have limited options. Propcash flips that dynamic: instead of one company making you a take-it-or-leave-it offer, multiple investors compete to win your property.
