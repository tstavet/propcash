Why Tennessee

Why Tennessee is one of America's hottest investor markets

Tennessee attracts more investor interest than almost any other state. For you as a seller, that means more buyers competing for your property — and better offers.

No State Income Tax

Tennessee is one of just 9 states with no income tax. Investors keep more of their returns, which makes them willing to pay more for Tennessee properties.

Explosive Population Growth

Tennessee's population is growing at 2x the national average. People are moving from California, New York, and Illinois — and they all need housing.

Strong Rental Returns

Memphis offers 9-12% rental yields. Nashville averages 7-9%. Investors actively seek Tennessee properties because the returns outperform most of the country.

Frequently asked questions about selling in Tennessee

Do I have to pay state income tax when I sell my Tennessee home?

No — Tennessee has no state income tax. This is one of the reasons the state attracts so many investors and new residents. When you sell your home, you won't owe Tennessee any state income tax on your proceeds. You may still owe federal capital gains tax depending on your situation, but consult a tax professional for advice specific to your circumstances.

What Tennessee cities do you cover?

We have active investor networks across the state. Currently, our strongest coverage is in Nashville (including Antioch, East Nashville, Madison, Hermitage, and surrounding areas). We're expanding to Memphis and Knoxville. We also serve Chattanooga, Clarksville, Murfreesboro, Franklin, and many smaller Tennessee cities. Submit your property to see if we have investors interested in your area.

How fast can you close on a Tennessee property?

Tennessee doesn't require attorney involvement in real estate closings, which speeds things up compared to states that do. Most of our Tennessee sales close in 10-14 days. When sellers need maximum speed, we've closed in as few as 7 days. You can also request a longer timeline if you need time to find your next home or coordinate logistics.

Why are so many investors buying Tennessee properties?

Tennessee offers investors a rare combination: no state income tax, strong population growth (2x the national average), affordable entry prices compared to coastal states, and high rental yields. Memphis delivers 9-12% rental yields — among the best in the country. Nashville offers appreciation potential. Knoxville provides stable university-driven demand. This investor interest translates to more buyers competing for your property.

What's the difference between selling in Nashville vs Memphis vs Knoxville?

Each market attracts different types of investors. Nashville draws investors seeking appreciation and higher-end rentals — it's Tennessee's hottest market with the highest prices (~$450K median). Memphis attracts cash-flow investors because of its affordability (~$190K median) and double-digit rental yields. Knoxville appeals to investors seeking steady growth and student housing near the University of Tennessee. All three markets have strong investor demand.

My property needs significant repairs. Will Tennessee investors still buy it?

Absolutely — Tennessee's investor-friendly environment means our buyers actively seek properties that need work. They're in the business of renovation. Outdated kitchens, old roofs, foundation issues, deferred maintenance — investors budget for all of it. Sell as-is and let them handle the updates.

Are there any fees or commissions?

Zero. No realtor commissions (typically 5-6% in Tennessee), no closing costs, no hidden fees. Propcash is completely free for Tennessee sellers. The cash offer you accept is exactly what you receive at closing.

Traditional "we buy houses" companies make you one offer and hope you accept — often a lowball. Propcash is a marketplace that sends your property to 500+ investors who compete for it. More buyers = more competition = better offers. Tennessee sellers regularly tell us they received significantly more through our marketplace than from single-buyer offers.

Investors often specialize in specific markets, so your Nashville property will go to Nashville-focused investors while your Memphis property goes to Memphis investors. Each will receive competing offers from buyers who know that specific market.
