The Problem We Saw

In 2019, a family member was trying to sell an inherited property. They contacted several "we buy houses" companies, hoping to get fair offers.

Every single one lowballed them. The offers ranged from 60-70% of market value, with no explanation for how they arrived at those numbers.

The problem wasn't the offers themselves‚Äîit was the lack of competition. With only one buyer giving one offer, sellers had no leverage and no way to know if the price was fair.

That's when we realized: the cash home buying industry needed a marketplace.

How We Make Money (And Why It Matters)

Transparency is core to what we do, so let's be clear about our business model:

üí∞

Platform Fee from Investors

When an investor successfully closes on a property through our marketplace, they pay us a platform fee (typically 2-3% of the sale price). This is similar to how Airbnb charges hosts, or how StockX charges sellers.

üéØ

Sellers Pay Nothing

You never pay us a fee. Not to use the platform, not to receive offers, not when you close. The service is completely free for sellers because investors cover the platform cost.

Why This Model Works Better

Because we only get paid when deals close successfully, our incentives are aligned with yours. We're motivated to create competition and get you multiple strong offers‚Äînot to lowball you on a single offer. The better the offers you get, the more likely a deal happens, and the more successful we are.
